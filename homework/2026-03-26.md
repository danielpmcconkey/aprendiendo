## Part 1: Articles and Set Phrases (8 points)

For each sentence, fill in the blank with the correct combination: **a la**, **al**, **a**, **de la**, **del**, **de**, **en la**, **en el**, **en**, or **nothing** (just the word after the blank, no article needed).

Some nouns drop the article in set phrases — *a casa* ("home"), *en casa* ("at home"), *a misa* ("to mass"). Most nouns keep theirs. The question is always: is this a frozen idiom, or a regular noun that follows normal rules?

1. Cada mañana, yo caminaba ___a la_____ escuela.

2. Después de clase, volvía ___a_____ casa.

3. Mi abuela vivía cerca ____de la____ playa.

4. Los domingos íbamos ____a la____ iglesia.

5. Mi padre trabajaba ____en____ centro de la ciudad.

6. Por la noche, todos estábamos __en______ casa.

7. El taxi nos llevó ___al_____ hotel.

8. Salimos ___al_____ aeropuerto muy temprano.

## Part 2: Que vs Lo que (6 points)

Complete each sentence with **que** or **lo que**.

The rule: if you can substitute "**that**" in English, use **que**. If you can substitute "**what**" or "**that which**," use **lo que**.

1. Sabía ___que_____ no hablábamos español muy bien.

2. No entendí __lo que_______ dijo el taxista.

3. ____lo que____ más me gustó de Granada fue la Alhambra.

4. Mi esposa me dijo ____que____ el hotel era bonito.

5. Eso es exactamente ____lo que____ quiero decir.

6. Creo ____que____ vamos a volver algún día.

## Part 3: Verb + Preposition + Infinitive (8 points)

Many Spanish verbs require a specific preposition before an infinitive. English doesn't always signal which one — you have to know the pairing.

**Reference — the 8 patterns in this exercise:**

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| aprender **a** + inf | to learn to | Aprendí a nadar. |
| empezar **a** + inf | to start/begin to | Empezó a llover. |
| dejar **de** + inf | to stop/quit | Dejó de fumar. |
| tratar **de** + inf | to try to | Traté de entender. |
| ir **a** + inf | to be going to | Vamos a comer. |
| tener **que** + inf | to have to | Tengo que estudiar. |
| acabar **de** + inf | to have just (done) | Acabo de llegar. |
| soñar **con** + inf | to dream of (doing) | Sueño con viajar. |

Translate each sentence into Spanish. You must use the correct verb + preposition pattern **and** choose the right tense (imperfect or preterite). One point per sentence — both the preposition and the tense must be correct for full credit, half credit for one but not the other.

1. She learned to cook when she was young.
Ella aprendía a cocinar quando era joven

2. We started to speak in Spanish with the taxi driver.
Empezamos a hablar en Español con el taxista

3. He stopped smoking five years ago.
El dejó de fumar hace cinco años.

4. I tried to understand, but he was speaking too fast.
Traté de entenderlo, pero hubo hablado demasiado rapido.

5. Every summer, we used to go visit our grandparents.
Cada verado, íbamos a visitar nuestros abuelos. [Dan note: my brain told me to use solíamos visitar but I know the assignment wanted me to use íbamos a visitar]

6. I had to work every Saturday.
Tenía que trabajar cada sábado.

7. She had just arrived when it started to rain.

## Part 4: Writing — Closed Book (8 points)

Write a short paragraph (6–7 sentences) about **what your life was like as a kid growing up near Tijuana**. Focus on what was habitual or ongoing (imperfect) versus specific things that happened (preterite).

**Requirements** (these count toward your grade):
- At least 3 imperfect verbs and 2 preterite verbs
- At least 2 different verb + preposition + infinitive constructions from the Part 3 reference list
- At least 1 use of *que* and 1 use of *lo que*

- Tense usage (imperfect vs preterite): 3 points

Cuando tenía un año y media, mi familia ha movado de Japón a San Diego. Nací en Japón, per creo que mi infancia era vivir en San Diego. Durante vente años, íba a las escuelas, conociba muchos amigos, aprendiba a tocar la guitarra, y, generalmente, tenía mucho felíz.

Yo sé lo que la significa a nuestra cultura. Soy emocionado, pero, también, tengo un poco miedo.
