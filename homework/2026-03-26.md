## Part 1: Articles and Set Phrases (8 points)

For each sentence, fill in the blank with the correct combination: **a la**, **al**, **a**, **de la**, **del**, **de**, **en la**, **en el**, **en**, or **nothing** (just the word after the blank, no article needed).

Some nouns drop the article in set phrases — *a casa* ("home"), *en casa* ("at home"), *a misa* ("to mass"). Most nouns keep theirs. The question is always: is this a frozen idiom, or a regular noun that follows normal rules?

1. Cada mañana, yo caminaba ________ escuela.

2. Después de clase, volvía ________ casa.

3. Mi abuela vivía cerca ________ playa.

4. Los domingos íbamos ________ iglesia.

5. Mi padre trabajaba ________ centro de la ciudad.

6. Por la noche, todos estábamos ________ casa.

7. El taxi nos llevó ________ hotel.

8. Salimos ________ aeropuerto muy temprano.

## Part 2: Que vs Lo que (6 points)

Complete each sentence with **que** or **lo que**.

The rule: if you can substitute "**that**" in English, use **que**. If you can substitute "**what**" or "**that which**," use **lo que**.

1. Sabía ________ no hablábamos español muy bien.

2. No entendí ________ dijo el taxista.

3. ________ más me gustó de Granada fue la Alhambra.

4. Mi esposa me dijo ________ el hotel era bonito.

5. Eso es exactamente ________ quiero decir.

6. Creo ________ vamos a volver algún día.

## Part 3: Verb + Preposition + Infinitive (8 points)

Many Spanish verbs require a specific preposition before an infinitive. English doesn't always signal which one — you have to know the pairing.

**Reference — the 8 patterns in this exercise:**

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| aprender **a** + inf | to learn to | Aprendí a nadar. |
| empezar **a** + inf | to start/begin to | Empezó a llover. |
| dejar **de** + inf | to stop/quit | Dejó de fumar. |
| tratar **de** + inf | to try to | Traté de entender. |
| ir **a** + inf | to be going to | Vamos a comer. |
| tener **que** + inf | to have to | Tengo que estudiar. |
| acabar **de** + inf | to have just (done) | Acabo de llegar. |
| soñar **con** + inf | to dream of (doing) | Sueño con viajar. |

Translate each sentence into Spanish. You must use the correct verb + preposition pattern **and** choose the right tense (imperfect or preterite). One point per sentence — both the preposition and the tense must be correct for full credit, half credit for one but not the other.

1. She learned to cook when she was young.

2. We started to speak in Spanish with the taxi driver.

3. He stopped smoking five years ago.

4. I tried to understand, but he was speaking too fast.

5. Every summer, we used to go visit our grandparents.

6. I had to work every Saturday.

7. She had just arrived when it started to rain.

8. As a child, I used to dream of living in Spain.
