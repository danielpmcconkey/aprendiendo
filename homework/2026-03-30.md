## Part 1: Read (10-15 minutes)

Look up in the Routledge: **por and para**. The book has a dedicated section comparing them.

Pay attention to the core distinction: **para** points forward (purpose, destination, recipient, deadline) and **por** points backward (cause, reason, exchange, movement through). Note any categories that surprise you.

## Part 2: Por vs Para — Fill the Blank (14 points)

Choose **por** or **para**. One point each.

7. El tren pasa ________por__ tres ciudades antes de llegar a Madrid.

8. _____para_____ mí, la mejor comida es la mexicana.

10. Lo hice ___para_______ ayudarte, no ____por______ obligación.

13. ¿_____para_____ qué sirve esta herramienta?

## Part 3: Translation — English to Spanish (16 points)

Translate each sentence. Focus on getting por/para right, but full marks require correct grammar and spelling throughout. Two points each.

1. I bought this book for my father.
Compré este libro para mi padre.

2. We walked through the city for three hours.
Andábamos a través de la ciudad por tres horas.

3. She works for a hospital in Barcelona.
Ella trabaja para un hospitál en Barcelona.

4. Thank you for the dinner.
Gracias por la cena.

5. I need to finish this by Monday.
Necesito terminarlo para el lunes.

6. For me, Arsenal are frauds.
Para mí, Arsenal son impostores. (¡Punta basura roja!)

7. He studied a lot in order to pass the exam.
Él estudió mucho para aprobar el examen.

8. They paid fifty euros for the tickets.
Pagaron cincuenta euros por las billetes. [Dan question. This seems like it should be para to me but deepL says to use por. they paid *in order to get* the tickets. Para, no?]
