## Systematic Error: -iba / -ía

The biggest finding. Dan applies -ar imperfect endings (-aba) to -er/-ir verbs in writing:

- conociba → conocía
- aprendiba → aprendía
- Also the reverse: sonía → soñaba (-ar verb getting -ía ending)

This doesn't appear in drills — only under writing pressure. Batch 03 includes 7 targeted cards for this pattern.

## Additional Error Patterns

- **Ser/estar under pressure:** Baseline was 100%, but "soy emocionado" and "no son solo" appeared in the 26th's writing. Production gap, not knowledge gap.
- **Missing prepositions in fixed constructions:** acabar **de** + inf, un poco **de**, cerca **de**
- **mucho vs muy:** Wrote "mucho felíz" — mucho before nouns, muy before adjectives/adverbs
- **hacer preterite stem:** hacimos → hicimos (hic-/hiz- stem)
- **mudarse:** Wrote "movar" — the verb doesn't exist. mudarse = to move residence.
- **Spelling under pressure:** quando, verado, solomente, vistarlos, lingua, prifieren, hispanohablantes

## Framework Changes (2026-03-27)

**Routledge reading component added to homework.** Dan owns *A New Reference Grammar of Modern Spanish* (6th ed, Butt & Benjamin). Each homework now includes a lookup task: a specific construction or rule to find in the Routledge and read (10-15 min) before doing drills. No chapter numbers — the lookup is itself a skill.

**Revised homework structure:**
1. Read (10-15 min) — Routledge lookup
2. Drill (10-15 min) — exercises testing what was just read
3. Write (10 min, closed book) — production under pressure

**Why:** Dan diagnosed the comprehensible-input gap: strong intuition, weak rule knowledge. He needs the "why" behind grammar, and rote drilling without understanding doesn't stick. The Routledge provides systematic explanations by linguists — exactly what his learning style demands.

## Grading Lessons Learned

From the 2026-03-25 session — Dan challenged three grading decisions and was right on all three:
1. Exercise constraints must be stated in the instructions, not in post-hoc "hints"
2. Ambiguous sentences (no habitual marker) shouldn't be marked wrong for a valid alternate reading
3. Error-spotting exercises should test what the lesson is about, not unrelated topics

These are standing orders for homework design going forward.
