# --- Conditional: regular formation ---
# Regular conditional = full infinitive + -ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían
Yo ______ (hablar, conditional) con él, pero no tengo su número.	<b>hablaría</b> — conditional yo of <b>hablar</b><br><i>I would talk to him, but I don't have his number.</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>hablar</b> (REGULAR — infinitive + endings):<br>yo hablar<b>ía</b><br>tú hablar<b>ías</b><br>él/ella/ud. hablar<b>ía</b><br>nosotros hablar<b>íamos</b><br>vosotros hablar<b>íais</b><br>ellos/ellas/uds. hablar<b>ían</b><br><br><i>Conditional = infinitive + endings that look exactly like -er/-ir imperfect endings (-ía, -ías, etc.). Same endings for ALL verb classes. Same irregular stems as the future tense.</i>	phase1 verbs conditional hablar regular
¿Tú ______ (comer, conditional) en ese restaurante otra vez?	<b>comerías</b> — conditional tú of <b>comer</b><br><i>Would you eat at that restaurant again?</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>comer</b> (REGULAR):<br>yo comer<b>ía</b><br>tú comer<b>ías</b><br>él/ella/ud. comer<b>ía</b><br>nosotros comer<b>íamos</b><br>vosotros comer<b>íais</b><br>ellos/ellas/uds. comer<b>ían</b><br><br><i>All accented on the í. Regular conditional = infinitive + the endings. Works for -ar, -er, -ir identically.</i>	phase1 verbs conditional comer regular
Nosotros ______ (vivir, conditional) en España si pudiéramos.	<b>viviríamos</b> — conditional nosotros of <b>vivir</b><br><i>We would live in Spain if we could.</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>vivir</b> (REGULAR):<br>yo vivir<b>ía</b><br>tú vivir<b>ías</b><br>él/ella/ud. vivir<b>ía</b><br>nosotros vivir<b>íamos</b><br>vosotros vivir<b>íais</b><br>ellos/ellas/uds. vivir<b>ían</b><br><br><i>-ir verbs: same pattern. Full infinitive + conditional endings. No exceptions for regulars.</i>	phase1 verbs conditional vivir regular
# --- Conditional: irregular stems (same as future!) ---
Yo ______ (tener, conditional) más paciencia si no estuviera tan cansado.	<b>tendría</b> — conditional yo of <b>tener</b><br><i>I would have more patience if I weren't so tired.</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>tener</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>tendr-</b> — same as future):<br>yo <b>tendr</b>ía<br>tú <b>tendr</b>ías<br>él/ella/ud. <b>tendr</b>ía<br>nosotros <b>tendr</b>íamos<br>vosotros <b>tendr</b>íais<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>tendr</b>ían<br><br><i>Every irregular conditional stem = its irregular future stem. If you know tendré (future), you know tendría (conditional). Always.</i>	phase1 verbs conditional tener irregular
¿Tú ______ (poder, conditional) ayudarme con esto?	<b>podrías</b> — conditional tú of <b>poder</b><br><i>Could you help me with this?</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>poder</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>podr-</b>):<br>yo <b>podr</b>ía<br>tú <b>podr</b>ías<br>él/ella/ud. <b>podr</b>ía<br>nosotros <b>podr</b>íamos<br>vosotros <b>podr</b>íais<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>podr</b>ían<br><br><i>podr- for both future (podré) and conditional (podría). The ending tells you the tense. "Podría" is the single most useful conditional form — "could you / I could."</i>	phase1 verbs conditional poder irregular
Ella ______ (hacer, conditional) la cena, pero no tiene tiempo.	<b>haría</b> — conditional él/ella/ud. of <b>hacer</b><br><i>She would make dinner, but she doesn't have time.</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>hacer</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>har-</b>):<br>yo <b>har</b>ía<br>tú <b>har</b>ías<br>él/ella/ud. <b>har</b>ía<br>nosotros <b>har</b>íamos<br>vosotros <b>har</b>íais<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>har</b>ían<br><br><i>har- for both future (haré) and conditional (haría). Unique stem — entire -cer drops.</i>	phase1 verbs conditional hacer irregular
¿Qué le ______ (decir, conditional, tú) a tu jefe en esa situación?	<b>dirías</b> — conditional tú of <b>decir</b><br><i>What would you say to your boss in that situation?</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>decir</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>dir-</b>):<br>yo <b>dir</b>ía<br>tú <b>dir</b>ías<br>él/ella/ud. <b>dir</b>ía<br>nosotros <b>dir</b>íamos<br>vosotros <b>dir</b>íais<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>dir</b>ían<br><br><i>dir- for both future (diré) and conditional (diría). Unique stem — most of the infinitive drops.</i>	phase1 verbs conditional decir irregular
Nosotros ______ (salir, conditional) más temprano, pero llovía mucho.	<b>saldríamos</b> — conditional nosotros of <b>salir</b><br><i>We would have left earlier, but it was raining a lot.</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>salir</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>saldr-</b>):<br>yo <b>saldr</b>ía<br>tú <b>saldr</b>ías<br>él/ella/ud. <b>saldr</b>ía<br>nosotros <b>saldr</b>íamos<br>vosotros <b>saldr</b>íais<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>saldr</b>ían<br><br><i>Replace vowel with -d-. Same group as tener (tendr-), poner (pondr-), venir (vendr-).</i>	phase1 verbs conditional salir irregular
Yo ______ (querer, conditional) ir a la playa, pero tengo que trabajar.	<b>querría</b> — conditional yo of <b>querer</b><br><i>I would like to go to the beach, but I have to work.</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>querer</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>querr-</b>):<br>yo <b>querr</b>ía<br>tú <b>querr</b>ías<br>él/ella/ud. <b>querr</b>ía<br>nosotros <b>querr</b>íamos<br>vosotros <b>querr</b>íais<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>querr</b>ían<br><br><i>Drop the -e-. querr- for both future and conditional. "Querría" = "I would like" — more polite than "quiero."</i>	phase1 verbs conditional querer irregular
Él ______ (venir, conditional) a la fiesta si lo invitaran.	<b>vendría</b> — conditional él/ella/ud. of <b>venir</b><br><i>He would come to the party if they invited him.</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>venir</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>vendr-</b>):<br>yo <b>vendr</b>ía<br>tú <b>vendr</b>ías<br>él/ella/ud. <b>vendr</b>ía<br>nosotros <b>vendr</b>íamos<br>vosotros <b>vendr</b>íais<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>vendr</b>ían<br><br><i>Replace vowel with -d-. vendr- for both future (vendrá) and conditional (vendría).</i>	phase1 verbs conditional venir irregular
Nosotros ______ (poner, conditional) la mesa, pero no sabemos dónde están los platos.	<b>pondríamos</b> — conditional nosotros of <b>poner</b><br><i>We would set the table, but we don't know where the plates are.</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>poner</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>pondr-</b>):<br>yo <b>pondr</b>ía<br>tú <b>pondr</b>ías<br>él/ella/ud. <b>pondr</b>ía<br>nosotros <b>pondr</b>íamos<br>vosotros <b>pondr</b>íais<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>pondr</b>ían<br><br><i>poner across 3 tenses: preterite (pus-), future (pondr-), conditional (pondr-). Future and conditional always share stems.</i>	phase1 verbs conditional poner irregular
______ (haber, conditional, impersonal) más opciones si llegaras más temprano.	<b>habría</b> — conditional impersonal of <b>haber</b><br><i>There would be more options if you arrived earlier.</i><br><br>Conditional of <b>haber</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>habr-</b>):<br>yo <b>habr</b>ía<br>tú <b>habr</b>ías<br>él/ella/ud. <b>habr</b>ía<br>nosotros <b>habr</b>íamos<br>vosotros <b>habr</b>íais<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>habr</b>ían<br><br><i>Impersonal haber in all tenses: hay (present), hubo (preterite), había (imperfect), habrá (future), habría (conditional). Always 3rd person singular.</i>	phase1 verbs conditional haber irregular impersonal
# --- Vocabulary: high-frequency words from homework gaps ---
todavía / aún	<b>todavía</b> / <b>aún</b> = <i>still, yet</i><br><br>• <b>Todavía</b> estoy esperando. = I'm still waiting.<br>• ¿<b>Todavía</b> no has comido? = You haven't eaten yet?<br>• <b>Aún</b> no sé. = I still don't know.<br><br><i>Todavía and aún are interchangeable. "Todavía no" / "aún no" = not yet.</i>	phase1 vocab adverbs
ya	<b>ya</b> = <i>already, now, anymore</i><br><br>• <b>Ya</b> comí. = I already ate.<br>• <b>Ya</b> voy. = I'm coming (right now).<br>• <b>Ya</b> no vivo allí. = I don't live there anymore.<br><br><i>Meaning shifts with tense: past = already, present = now/right away, negative = anymore. Extremely common.</i>	phase1 vocab adverbs
aunque	<b>aunque</b> = <i>although, even though, even if</i><br><br>• Fui a trabajar <b>aunque</b> estaba enfermo. = I went to work even though I was sick.<br>• <b>Aunque</b> llueva, vamos a ir. = Even if it rains, we're going to go.<br><br><i>With indicative = "even though" (fact). With subjunctive = "even if" (hypothetical). One of the key subjunctive triggers.</i>	phase1 vocab conjunctions subjunctive-preview
sin embargo	<b>sin embargo</b> = <i>however, nevertheless</i><br><br>• El hotel era caro. <b>Sin embargo</b>, la ubicación era perfecta. = The hotel was expensive. However, the location was perfect.<br><br><i>Formal connector. Usually starts a new sentence or follows a semicolon. More formal than "pero."</i>	phase1 vocab conjunctions
mientras	<b>mientras</b> = <i>while, whereas</i><br><br>• Ella cocinaba <b>mientras</b> yo ponía la mesa. = She was cooking while I set the table.<br>• <b>Mientras</b> tanto = meanwhile<br><br><i>Often paired with imperfect (background action) + preterite (foreground event). Key word for tense selection in narratives.</i>	phase1 vocab conjunctions
en cuanto / tan pronto como	<b>en cuanto</b> / <b>tan pronto como</b> = <i>as soon as</i><br><br>• <b>En cuanto</b> llegué, la llamé. = As soon as I arrived, I called her.<br>• <b>En cuanto</b> llegue, la llamaré. = As soon as I arrive, I'll call her.<br><br><i>Past = indicative. Future = subjunctive (because the event hasn't happened yet). Major subjunctive trigger.</i>	phase1 vocab conjunctions subjunctive-preview
por lo menos / al menos	<b>por lo menos</b> / <b>al menos</b> = <i>at least</i><br><br>• Necesitas <b>por lo menos</b> dos horas. = You need at least two hours.<br>• <b>Al menos</b> llegamos a tiempo. = At least we arrived on time.<br><br><i>Interchangeable. "Al menos" is slightly more common in writing.</i>	phase1 vocab adverbs
además	<b>además</b> = <i>besides, in addition, moreover</i><br><br>• No quiero ir. <b>Además</b>, no tengo tiempo. = I don't want to go. Besides, I don't have time.<br>• <b>Además de</b> español, habla francés. = In addition to Spanish, he speaks French.<br><br><i>"Además" alone = moreover/besides. "Además de" + noun = in addition to.</i>	phase1 vocab conjunctions
apenas	<b>apenas</b> = <i>barely, hardly, as soon as</i><br><br>• <b>Apenas</b> pude dormir anoche. = I barely slept last night.<br>• <b>Apenas</b> llegó, empezó a llover. = As soon as he arrived, it started to rain.<br><br><i>Two meanings depending on context: "barely/hardly" (most common) or "as soon as" (narrative, more literary).</i>	phase1 vocab adverbs
en vez de / en lugar de	<b>en vez de</b> / <b>en lugar de</b> = <i>instead of</i><br><br>• <b>En vez de</b> manejar, tomamos el tren. = Instead of driving, we took the train.<br>• Compré café <b>en lugar de</b> té. = I bought coffee instead of tea.<br><br><i>Both followed by noun or infinitive. Interchangeable. "En vez de" is more colloquial.</i>	phase1 vocab prepositions
darse cuenta (de)	<b>darse cuenta (de)</b> = <i>to realize</i><br><br>• Me <b>di cuenta de</b> que era tarde. = I realized it was late.<br>• No se <b>dio cuenta</b>. = He/she didn't realize.<br><br><i>Reflexive. "Darse cuenta de que" (not "darse cuenta que" — the de is required). Don't confuse with "realizar" which means "to carry out/accomplish," NOT "to realize."</i>	phase1 vocab verbs reflexive
a pesar de	<b>a pesar de</b> = <i>despite, in spite of</i><br><br>• <b>A pesar de</b> la lluvia, salimos a caminar. = Despite the rain, we went out for a walk.<br>• <b>A pesar de que</b> estaba cansado, terminé el proyecto. = Even though I was tired, I finished the project.<br><br><i>"A pesar de" + noun. "A pesar de que" + conjugated verb. Similar to "aunque" but more emphatic.</i>	phase1 vocab prepositions
