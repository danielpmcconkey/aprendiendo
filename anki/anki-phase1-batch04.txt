# --- por vs para (production) ---
# Baseline 70%, confirmed shaky in writing. "por la cena" (purpose) should have been "para la cena."
Compré flores ______ mi madre.	Compré flores <b>para</b> mi madre.<br><i>I bought flowers for my mother.</i><br><br><i><b>para</b> = recipient / destination. "For whom?" = para.</i>	phase1 grammar por-para
Estudié mucho ______ el examen.	Estudié mucho <b>para</b> el examen.<br><i>I studied a lot for the exam.</i><br><br><i><b>para</b> = purpose / goal. Studying with the goal of passing. NOT por.</i>	phase1 grammar por-para
Preparé la mesa ______ la cena.	Preparé la mesa <b>para</b> la cena.<br><i>I set the table for dinner.</i><br><br><i><b>para</b> = purpose. The table is FOR the dinner (its goal/destination). NOT por.</i>	phase1 grammar por-para
Gracias ______ tu ayuda.	Gracias <b>por</b> tu ayuda.<br><i>Thanks for your help.</i><br><br><i><b>por</b> = cause / reason. Thanking BECAUSE OF your help. Gratitude = por.</i>	phase1 grammar por-para
Caminamos ______ el parque durante una hora.	Caminamos <b>por</b> el parque durante una hora.<br><i>We walked through the park for an hour.</i><br><br><i><b>por</b> = movement through/along. Para = destination (toward).</i>	phase1 grammar por-para
Lo hice ______ ti.	Lo hice <b>por</b> ti.<br><i>I did it because of you / for your sake.</i><br><br><i><b>por</b> = on behalf of / because of. "Lo hice para ti" = I made it for you (recipient). Different meaning.</i>	phase1 grammar por-para
Purpose vs cause:<br>"Trabajo ______ vivir" (in order to live)<br>"Trabajo ______ necesidad" (because of necessity)	"Trabajo <b>para</b> vivir" (in order to live — purpose/goal)<br>"Trabajo <b>por</b> necesidad" (because of necessity — cause/reason)<br><br><i>The core distinction: <b>para</b> = where it's going (purpose, destination, recipient, deadline). <b>por</b> = where it came from (cause, reason, exchange, movement through).</i>	phase1 grammar por-para rule
# --- ser vs haber for existence ("there were") ---
# "era doce invitados" → "había doce invitados." Ser ≠ haber for "there were."
______ (there were) muchas personas en la fiesta.	<b>Había</b> muchas personas en la fiesta.<br><i>There were many people at the party.</i><br><br><i>haber (impersonal) = there is/are/was/were. hay (present), había (imperfect), hubo (preterite). NEVER use ser for "there were."</i>	phase1 grammar haber-existential
______ (there were) doce invitados en la cena.	<b>Había</b> doce invitados en la cena.<br><i>There were twelve guests at dinner.</i><br><br><i>había — always singular, even with plural nouns. "Había doce," NOT "habían doce" (common error, even among native speakers).</i>	phase1 grammar haber-existential

# --- más needs accent ---
