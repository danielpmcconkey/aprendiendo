# --- Irregular preterite: poder (pud-) ---
No ______ (poder, preterite, yo) encontrar mis llaves en ninguna parte.	<b>pude</b> — preterite yo of <b>poder</b><br><i>I couldn't find my keys anywhere.</i><br><br>Preterite of <b>poder</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>pud-</b>):<br>yo <b>pud</b>e<br>tú <b>pud</b>iste<br>él/ella/ud. <b>pud</b>o<br>nosotros <b>pud</b>imos<br>vosotros <b>pud</b>isteis<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>pud</b>ieron<br><br><i>No accents on any form. Preterite of poder often means "managed to" (affirmative) or "couldn't" (negative) — a specific occasion, not ongoing ability.</i>	phase1 verbs preterite poder

# --- Irregular preterite: hacer (hic-/hiz-) ---

# --- Irregular preterite: decir ellos (i-drop after j) ---
Mis amigos me ______ (decir, preterite, ellos) que la película era muy buena.	<b>dijeron</b> — preterite ellos/ellas/uds. of <b>decir</b><br><i>My friends told me the movie was very good.</i><br><br>Preterite of <b>decir</b> (IRREGULAR stem: <b>dij-</b>):<br>yo <b>dij</b>e<br>tú <b>dij</b>iste<br>él/ella/ud. <b>dij</b>o<br>nosotros <b>dij</b>imos<br>vosotros <b>dij</b>isteis<br>ellos/ellas/uds. <b>dij</b>eron (NOT dijieron)<br><br><i>After j, the i in -ieron drops → -eron. # --- Gender agreement: -o/-a adjectives with feminine nouns ---

El agua estaba muy ______ (cold, adj. agreeing with "agua") esa mañana.	<b>fría</b> — frío → frí<b>a</b> (fem., agreeing with <b>agua</b>)<br><i>The water was very cold that morning.</i><br><br><i>agua is feminine despite using "el" (el agua, but esta agua, agua fría). The article switches to el before stressed a-, but the noun stays feminine.</i>	phase1 grammar agreement gender
La comida ______ (Mexican, adj. agreeing with "comida") es mi favorita.	<b>mexicana</b> — mexicano → mexican<b>a</b> (fem., agreeing with <b>comida</b>)<br><i>Mexican food is my favorite.</i><br><br><i>Nationality adjectives ending in -o follow the same -o → -a pattern.
